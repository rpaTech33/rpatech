What Do Robotic Process Automation (RPA) Consultants Do?

Robotic Process Automation consultants use proven technological developments to manipulate application software to perform processes in a similar way a human would do. They explore possibilities in teaching robots how to accomplish human tasks in a faster and more accurate way. With the wealth of experience from working on complex projects, the RPA consultant carries the organization through planning, definition, design configuration, testing, and operations changeover.

The need to drive faster and more accurate processes is gaining momentum. To meet the growing market demands, businesses are affecting process changes by augmenting human activity with automation. With efficiency being at the core of improving business processes, companies can meet their objectives by implementing methods that relate business needs with automation solutions. RPA consultants create robots that accelerate day-to-day employee tasks thus achieving efficiency.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why should I start using RPA software ?

To answer this question, we should briefly explain what RPA is. RPA refers to software robots that take over simple, repetitive tasks from humans. For example, human actions like entering, retyping, or copying information. RPA software can perform these tasks quicker, more accurately, and at a higher level of quality than humans would. The advantages are higher customer and employee satisfaction, a decrease in processing time, and lower working capacity.

What are the challenges of RPA ?

There are multiple challenges that can appear when implementing RPA in your organization, here are three of the major and most common ones:
-Employee resistance and onboarding: Any technology implementation comes with changes that can result in stress for employees. In the case of RPA there is also the belief that bringing in automation, equals to layoffs, creating some adoption resistance.

-Selecting the right processes: It is vital to determine which processes are suitable for RPA so that automation runs successfully. This requires an in-depth analysis and should not be rushed to deploy, as this might lead to failure to meet the expectations.

-Setting realistic RPA expectations: Companies need to recognize the limits of what RPA can do and understand that RPA will not fix broken processes.

What is the added value of RPA ?

The added work capacity that RPA offers can be used for a variety of tasks. This allows employees to spend their time on tasks that interest them and that only they can perform. This way, you are optimizing your resources, ensuring all tasks are performed at a higher quality and in a more cost-effective manner.

What is the relationship between RPA & AI ?

RPA and AI are often named in the same conversation. Combining RPA and AI offers you the ideal combination. The collaboration between AI and RPA extends and improves the possibilities. Learning from every task. Alone, RPA does not have the cognitive capacity to take on any of the interpretations. The combination extends and improves the reach of RPA by allowing it to map business processes and automize processes at a more detailed level.

What about my employees ?

It is natural for your employees' first response to be shocked when they are told a robot will be taking over part of their work. However, in reality, the robot only takes over the work employees usually enjoy least. They are the repetitive and simple tasks that are least likely to energize employees. Eliminating these tasks provides employees with more time for fun and challenging work. By allowing employees to experience RPA, they are given the chance to see the opportunity it could offer them rather than the threat.
